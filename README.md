# Employee Attrition Analysis using Machine Learning

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by organizations. This project aims to predict whether an employee is likely to leave the company based on various factors such as age, job role, monthly income, overtime, work-life balance, and job satisfaction.

Machine Learning models were developed to analyze employee data and identify the key factors influencing employee attrition. The insights generated from this project can help HR departments improve employee retention strategies.

---

## 🎯 Objective

- Analyze employee attrition patterns.
- Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
- Build machine learning models to predict employee attrition.
- Compare model performance using evaluation metrics.
- Identify the most important factors contributing to employee turnover.

---

## 📂 Dataset

- **Dataset Name:** IBM HR Employee Attrition Dataset
- **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset (Kaggle)
- **Records:** 1470
- **Features:** 35

The dataset contains employee demographic, job-related, and performance-related information.

Random Forest Feature Importance was used to identify the most influential factors affecting employee attrition.

Some important features include:

- Monthly Income
- Overtime
- Age
- Years at Company
- Job Role
- Job Satisfaction

---

## 💡 Business Insights

- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the company.
- Lower job satisfaction is associated with higher attrition.
- Monthly income plays a significant role in employee retention.
- Employees with fewer years at the company show higher attrition rates.
- Certain job roles have higher employee turnover than others.
